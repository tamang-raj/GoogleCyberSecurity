## 📚 Glossary

### Assess
The **fifth step** of the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) used to determine whether implemented security controls are working correctly.

### Authorize
The **sixth step** of the NIST RMF where management accepts responsibility for the organization's security and privacy risks before systems become operational.

### Business Continuity
An organization's ability to continue normal business operations by implementing disaster recovery and continuity plans during disruptions.

### Categorize
The **second step** of the NIST RMF that identifies and classifies information systems based on their risk level and security requirements.

### External Threat
Any threat originating **outside** an organization that has the potential to damage assets, systems, or data.

### Implement
The **fourth step** of the NIST RMF that involves putting selected security and privacy controls into operation.

### Internal Threat
A security risk posed by someone **inside or trusted by** the organization, such as:
- Current employees
- Former employees
- Contractors
- Vendors
- Business partners

### Monitor
The **seventh and final step** of the NIST RMF that continuously evaluates systems to ensure security controls remain effective.

### Prepare
The **first step** of the NIST RMF that focuses on planning activities needed to manage security and privacy risks before incidents occur.

### Ransomware
A malicious attack in which threat actors encrypt an organization's data and demand payment (a ransom) to restore access.

### Risk
Anything that can negatively affect the **Confidentiality, Integrity, or Availability (CIA)** of an organization's assets.

### Risk Mitigation
The process of reducing the likelihood or impact of risks by implementing appropriate security procedures, policies, and controls.

### Security Posture
An organization's overall ability to protect critical assets, manage cybersecurity risks, and respond effectively to security incidents.

### Select
The **third step** of the NIST RMF where organizations choose, tailor, and document security controls that protect systems and data.

### Shared Responsibility
The principle that **everyone in an organization** plays a role in maintaining both physical and digital security.

### Social Engineering
A manipulation technique that exploits human psychology and trust to obtain confidential information, unauthorized access, or valuable assets.

### Vulnerability
A weakness in a system, process, or person that can be exploited by a threat actor.

---

## 📝 Key Takeaways

- The **NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)** consists of **7 steps**:
  1. Prepare
  2. Categorize
  3. Select
  4. Implement
  5. Assess
  6. Authorize
  7. Monitor

- **Risk** exists when a **threat** can exploit a **vulnerability**.

- **Ransomware** encrypts data and demands payment for recovery.

- **Social engineering** targets people rather than technology.

- Maintaining a strong **security posture** requires continuous monitoring and shared responsibility across the organization.

- **Business continuity** ensures organizations can continue operating during security incidents or disasters.
