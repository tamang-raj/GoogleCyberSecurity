# Question 1

## Question
**Which of the following statements accurately describe playbooks?** *(Select three answers.)*

### Correct Answers
- ✅ A playbook is an essential tool used in cybersecurity.
- ✅ A playbook can be used to respond to an incident.
- ✅ A playbook improves efficiency when identifying and mitigating an incident.

### Incorrect Option
- ❌ A playbook is used to develop compliance regulations.

### Explanation
Playbooks provide standardized procedures for responding to cybersecurity incidents. They improve consistency, efficiency, and compliance but **do not create compliance regulations**.

## Question

# Question 5

## Question
**What are the primary goals of the containment phase of an incident response playbook?** *(Select two answers.)*

### Correct Answers
- ✅ Prevent further damage.
- ✅ Reduce the immediate impact.

### Incorrect Options

# Question 8

## Question
**In what ways do SIEM tools and playbooks help security teams respond to an incident?** *(Select all that apply.)*

### Correct Answers
- ✅ SIEM tools and playbooks work together to provide an efficient way of handling security incidents.
- ✅ SIEM alerts inform security teams of potential threats.
- ✅ SIEM tools analyze data.

### ❌ Mistake Made
You selected:

- ❌ **SIEM alerts provide security teams with specific steps to identify and respond to security incidents.**

### Why It Is Incorrect

SIEM tools **only detect, collect, analyze, and generate alerts** based on security events.

**Playbooks** provide the **specific step-by-step procedures** for investigating, containing, recovering from, and documenting incidents.

**Remember:**
- **SIEM = Detects and Alerts**
- **Playbook = Guides the Response**

# Key Concepts

## Playbooks
- Living documents
- Standardized response procedures
- Improve efficiency
- Ensure consistency
- Updated after every major incident

## SIEM
- Collects log data
- Detects suspicious activity
- Generates alerts
- Analyzes security events
- Supports investigations

## Incident Response Phases

1. Preparation
2. Detection and Analysis
3. Containment
4. Eradication and Recovery
5. Post-Incident Activity
6. Coordination
