# Terms and Definitions – Course 2, Module 4

## Incident Response
**Definition:** An organization's quick attempt to identify an attack, contain the damage, and correct the effects of a security breach.

**Example:**
A security team detects ransomware on a company server. They immediately isolate the infected system, remove the malware, restore data from backups, and investigate the attack to prevent it from happening again.

---

## Playbook
**Definition:** A manual that provides details about any operational action.

**Example:**
A phishing incident playbook outlines every step a security analyst should follow, including:
- Verifying the phishing alert
- Investigating the affected user's account
- Containing the threat
- Removing malicious emails
- Recovering affected systems
- Documenting the incident
- Reporting findings to stakeholders

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Incident Response** focuses on quickly detecting, containing, eliminating, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents.
- **Playbooks** provide standardized, step-by-step procedures that help security teams respond consistently and efficiently.
- Playbooks are **living documents** and should be updated regularly to address new threats, vulnerabilities, and lessons learned.
