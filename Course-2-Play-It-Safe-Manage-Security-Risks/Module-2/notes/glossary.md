# 📚 Terms and Definitions – Course 2, Module 2

## A

### **Asset**
An item perceived as having value to an organization.

### **Attack Vectors**
The pathways attackers use to penetrate security defenses.

### **Authentication**
The process of verifying who someone is.

### **Authorization**
The concept of granting access to specific resources in a system.

### **Availability**
The principle that authorized users can access data whenever it is needed.

---

## B

### **Biometrics**
Unique physical characteristics (such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris scans) used to verify a person's identity.

---

## C

### **Confidentiality**
The principle that only authorized users can access specific assets or data.

### **Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA) Triad**
A foundational security model that helps organizations manage risk and design secure systems and security policies.

---

## D

### **Detect (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function focused on identifying potential security incidents and improving monitoring capabilities for faster detection.

---

## E

### **Encryption**
The process of converting readable data (plaintext) into an encoded, unreadable format (ciphertext) to protect confidentiality.

---

## G

### **Govern (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function that focuses on establishing, overseeing, and continuously improving an organization's cybersecurity strategy, policies, roles, and risk management processes to align with business goals and regulatory requirements.

---

## I

### **Identify (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function related to understanding and managing cybersecurity risks affecting an organization's people, assets, systems, and data.

### **Integrity**
The principle that data remains correct, authentic, accurate, and reliable throughout its lifecycle.

---

## N

### **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**
A voluntary framework consisting of standards, guidelines, and best practices that help organizations manage cybersecurity risks.

### **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Special Publication (SP) 800-53**
A comprehensive framework that provides security and privacy controls for protecting information systems within the U.S. federal government.

---

## O

### **Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP)**
A nonprofit organization dedicated to improving software security through open-source resources, best practices, and security principles.

---

## P

### **Protect (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function focused on implementing policies, procedures, training, and security tools to reduce cybersecurity threats.

---

## R

### **Recover (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function that focuses on restoring systems, services, and operations after a cybersecurity incident.

### **Respond (NIST CSF Function)**
A NIST Cybersecurity Framework function responsible for containing, analyzing, mitigating, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents while improving future response processes.

### **Risk**
Anything that can negatively affect the confidentiality, integrity, or availability (CIA) of an organization's assets.

---

## S

### **Security Audit**
A formal review of an organization's security controls, policies, and procedures to ensure they meet internal standards, industry best practices, and regulatory requirements.

### **Security Controls**
Safeguards implemented to reduce specific security risks. These can be administrative, technical, or physical controls.

### **Security Frameworks**
Structured guidelines used to develop security plans that help organizations reduce risks and protect data and privacy.

### **Security Posture**
An organization's overall ability to defend its critical assets, protect data, manage cybersecurity risks, and respond effectively to changes and threats.

---

## T

### **Threat**
Any circumstance, event, or actor that has the potential to negatively impact an organization's assets by exploiting vulnerabilities.

## 🎓 Module Summary

Course 2, Module 2 introduces the essential concepts used to build and maintain a strong cybersecurity program. It covers **security frameworks (NIST CSF, NIST SP 800-53, OWASP)**, **security controls**, the **CIA Triad**, **authentication and authorization**, **encryption**, and the six NIST Cybersecurity Framework functions (**Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover**). Together, these concepts help organizations protect valuable assets, manage cybersecurity risks, conduct security audits, and improve their overall security posture.
