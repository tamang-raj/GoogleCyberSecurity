# Terms and Definitions — Course 1, Module 1

## Cybersecurity (or Security)

**Definition:**  
The practice of ensuring the **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA)** of information by protecting networks, devices, people, and data from unauthorized access or criminal exploitation.

---

## Cloud Security

**Definition:**  
The process of ensuring that assets stored in the cloud are properly configured and that access to those assets is limited to authorized users.

---

## Internal Threat

**Definition:**  
A current or former employee, external vendor, or trusted partner who poses a security risk to an organization.

---

## Network Security

**Definition:**  
The practice of protecting an organization's network infrastructure from unauthorized access, misuse, or attacks.

---

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

**Definition:**  
Any information that can be used to identify or infer the identity of an individual.

**Examples:**
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Home address
- Date of birth

---

## Security Posture

**Definition:**  
An organization's overall ability to protect its critical assets and data while responding effectively to evolving security threats and changes.

---

## Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)

**Definition:**  
A specific category of **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** that requires stricter handling and protection due to its highly sensitive nature.

**Examples:**
- Social Security Number (SSN)
- Passport number
- Driver's license number
- Financial account information
- Medical records

---

## Technical Skills

**Definition:**  
Skills that require knowledge of specific tools, technologies, procedures, and security policies.

**Examples:**
- Linux
- Python
- SQL
- Network analysis
- Security tools

---

## Threat

**Definition:**  
Any circumstance, event, or action that has the potential to negatively impact an organization's assets, systems, or data.

---

## Threat Actor

**Definition:**  
Any individual or group that presents a security risk by attempting to exploit vulnerabilities or compromise systems.

**Examples:**
- Cybercriminals
- Hackers
- Nation-state attackers
- Insider threats
- Hacktivists

---

## Transferable Skills

**Definition:**  
Skills gained from previous experiences or careers that can be applied effectively in different roles or industries.

**Examples:**
- Communication
- Problem-solving
- Critical thinking
- Teamwork
- Time management
- Attention to detail

---
