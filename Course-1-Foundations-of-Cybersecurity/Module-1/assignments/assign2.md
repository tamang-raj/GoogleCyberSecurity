## Question 1

### Which of the following proficiencies are transferable skills, likely to be applicable in almost any field?

**Select three answers.**

### Correct Answers

- ✅ Problem-solving
- ✅ Written and verbal communication
- ✅ Analysis

### Explanation

**Transferable skills** are abilities that can be applied across many careers and industries.

These skills help cybersecurity professionals:

- Solve complex security challenges.
- Communicate effectively with technical and non-technical audiences.
- Analyze information to identify risks and vulnerabilities.

> **Programming** is a **technical skill**, not a transferable skill.

## Question 2

### Which of the following proficiencies are technical skills needed to become an entry-level security analyst?

**Select two answers.**

### Correct Answers

- ✅ Data analysis

### Explanation

Technical skills require specialized knowledge and hands-on experience.

Examples include:

- Programming languages (Python, Java, C++, etc.)

## Question 3

### Fill in the blank:

_____ identify, analyze, and preserve criminal evidence within networks, computers, and electronic devices.

### Correct Answer

✅ **Digital forensic investigators**

### Explanation

Digital forensic investigators are responsible for:

- Collecting digital evidence.
- Preserving evidence without altering it.
- Investigating cybercrimes.
- Recovering deleted or hidden data.
- Supporting legal investigations.

Their work is essential for incident response and cybercrime investigations.

## Question 4

### What are examples of Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII) that cybersecurity professionals need to protect?

**Select two answers.**

### Correct Answers

- ✅ Medical records
- ✅ Bank account numbers

### Explanation

**Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)** requires stronger protection because its exposure can cause significant harm.

Examples include:

- Medical records
- Bank account numbers
- Credit card information
- Social Security Numbers (SSN)
- Passport numbers
- Driver's license numbers
- Biometric information (fingerprints, facial recognition)

> **Email addresses** and **last names** are examples of **PII**, but they are generally **not classified as SPII**.
