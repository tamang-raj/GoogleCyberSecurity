# Question 3

### An employee receives an email that they believe to be legitimate. They click on a compromised link within the email.

What type of internal threat does this scenario describe?

### Correct Answer

✅ **Accidental**

### Explanation

An **accidental internal threat** occurs when a trusted individual unintentionally compromises security.

Examples include:

- Clicking phishing links
- Sending sensitive data to the wrong recipient
- Weak passwords
- Misconfigured systems

# Question 4

### What is identity theft?

### Correct Answer

✅ **Stealing personal information to commit fraud while impersonating a victim**

### Explanation

Identity theft occurs when attackers steal personal information such as:

- Social Security Numbers
- Bank account details
- Credit card information
- Login credentials

The stolen information is then used to commit fraud or impersonate the victim.

# Question 9

### Which statements accurately describe Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)?

**Select all that apply.**

### Correct Answers

- ✅ The theft of SPII is often more damaging than the theft of PII.
- ✅ SPII is a type of PII that falls under stricter handling guidelines.
- ✅ An example of PII is someone's email address.

### Explanation

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII is any information that can identify an individual.

**Examples:**

- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Home address
- Date of birth

---

### Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)

SPII is a more sensitive category of PII that requires stronger protection.

**Examples:**

- Social Security Number (SSN)
- Passport number
- Driver's license number
- Medical records
- Bank account information
- Credit card details
- Biometric data

Exposure of SPII can lead to identity theft, financial fraud, and serious privacy violations.
