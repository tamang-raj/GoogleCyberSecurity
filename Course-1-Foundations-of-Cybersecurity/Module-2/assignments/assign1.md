# Question 1

### Fill in the blank:

A computer virus is malicious _____ that interferes with computer operations and causes damage.

### Correct Answer

✅ **Code**

### Explanation

A **computer virus** is a type of **malware** (malicious software) consisting of malicious code designed to:

- Damage files or systems
- Corrupt data
- Disrupt normal computer operations
- Spread to other programs or devices

Unlike a worm, a virus typically requires user interaction (such as opening an infected file) to spread.

# Question 2

### What is one way that the Morris Worm helped shape the security industry?

### Correct Answer

✅ **It led to the development of computer response teams.**

### Explanation

The **Morris Worm** (1988) was one of the first major internet worms.

Its impact included:

- Exposing weaknesses in internet-connected systems.
- Causing widespread system outages.
- Leading to the creation of **Computer Security Incident Response Teams (CSIRTs)**.
- Increasing awareness of cybersecurity as an essential field.

# Question 3

### What were the key impacts of the Equifax breach?

**Select two answers.**

### Correct Answers

- ✅ Millions of customers' PII was stolen.
- ✅ The significant financial consequences of a breach became more apparent.

### Explanation

The **Equifax Data Breach (2017)** affected approximately **147 million** people.

Information exposed included:

- Names
- Social Security Numbers
- Birth dates
- Addresses
- Driver's license numbers

Major consequences included:

- Massive financial losses
- Legal settlements
- Loss of customer trust
- Increased focus on protecting Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

# Question 4

### Social engineering, such as phishing, is a manipulation technique that relies on computer error to gain private information, access, or valuables.

### Correct Answer

✅ **False**

### Explanation

Social engineering relies on **human error**, not computer error.

Attackers manipulate people into:

- Revealing passwords
- Clicking malicious links
- Downloading malware
- Sharing confidential information
- Granting unauthorized access

A common example is **phishing**, where attackers send fraudulent emails pretending to be trusted organizations.

# Module 2 Summary

## Malware

**Malware** is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Common types include:

- Virus
- Worm
- Trojan Horse
- Ransomware
- Spyware
- Adware

---

## Morris Worm (1988)

Key impacts:

- One of the first internet worms.
- Spread rapidly across connected systems.
- Inspired the creation of **Computer Security Incident Response Teams (CSIRTs)**.
- Increased cybersecurity awareness worldwide.

---

## Equifax Data Breach (2017)

Key facts:

- Approximately **147 million** individuals affected.
- Millions of customers' **PII** was exposed.
- Highlighted the financial and reputational consequences of poor cybersecurity.
- Reinforced the importance of protecting sensitive data.

---

## Social Engineering

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people to gain unauthorized access or information.

Common attacks include:

- Phishing
- Spear phishing
- Smishing (SMS phishing)
- Vishing (Voice phishing)
- Pretexting
- Baiting
- Tailgating

---

# Key Takeaways

- A **computer virus** is malicious code that damages systems and files.
- The **Morris Worm** led to the creation of **CSIRTs**.
- The **Equifax breach** demonstrated the severe financial and privacy impacts of data breaches.
- **Social engineering** exploits **human behavior**, not computer vulnerabilities.
