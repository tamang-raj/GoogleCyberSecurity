# The CIA Triad and NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

Organizations rely on foundational cybersecurity models and frameworks to protect their information systems and manage cybersecurity risks. Two of the most important are the **CIA Triad** and the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**.

---

# The CIA Triad

The **CIA Triad** is a foundational cybersecurity model that helps organizations design secure systems, manage risk, and develop security policies.

The three core principles are:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

Together, these principles ensure that information remains secure, accurate, and accessible.

---

# 1. Confidentiality

## Definition

**Confidentiality** ensures that only **authorized individuals** can access sensitive information and organizational assets.

The goal is to prevent unauthorized users from viewing confidential data.

---

## How Confidentiality Is Maintained

Organizations protect confidentiality using:

- Strong passwords
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Encryption
- Access Control Lists (ACLs)
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)

---

## Examples

- Only HR staff can access employee records.
- Only finance personnel can view payroll information.
- Customers can access only their own accounts.

---

# 2. Integrity

## Definition

**Integrity** ensures that data remains:

- Accurate
- Authentic
- Complete
- Reliable
- Unaltered

Information should not be modified by unauthorized users.

---

## How Integrity Is Protected

Common methods include:

- Hashing
- Digital signatures
- Checksums
- Version control
- File integrity monitoring
- Encryption

---

## Examples

- Preventing unauthorized changes to financial records.
- Verifying downloaded software has not been altered.
- Detecting unauthorized file modifications.

---

# 3. Availability

## Definition

**Availability** ensures that authorized users can access systems, applications, and data whenever needed.

Security is ineffective if legitimate users cannot access critical resources.

---

## How Availability Is Maintained

Organizations improve availability through:

- Regular backups
- Redundant systems
- Disaster recovery plans
- Load balancing
- System monitoring
- Hardware redundancy

---

## Examples

- Keeping websites online during heavy traffic.
- Restoring systems after hardware failures.
- Recovering data from backups after ransomware attacks.

---

# CIA Triad Summary

| Principle | Goal | Examples |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **Confidentiality** | Prevent unauthorized access | Passwords, MFA, encryption |
| **Integrity** | Keep data accurate, authentic, and reliable | Hashing, digital signatures |
| **Availability** | Ensure authorized access when needed | Backups, disaster recovery, redundant systems |

# Understanding Assets

## What Is an Asset?

An **asset** is anything an organization considers valuable.

Assets may be physical or digital.

---

## Examples of Assets

### Digital Assets

- Customer databases
- Applications
- Servers
- Source code
- Email systems
- Cloud storage

### Physical Assets

- Computers
- Mobile devices
- Network equipment
- Office buildings
- Data centers

---

# Asset Value

The value of an asset depends on the impact its loss, theft, or compromise would have on the organization.

High-value assets require stronger security controls.

---

## High-Value Assets

Examples include:

- Social Security numbers
- Financial records
- Medical information
- Customer databases
- Intellectual property

These assets require stricter protection because their compromise can result in significant financial, legal, and reputational damage.

---

## Lower-Value Assets

Examples include:

- Public marketing materials
- Public company websites
- Published reports

Although still protected, these assets generally require fewer security controls.

# The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

## Definition

The **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** is a voluntary framework that provides standards, guidelines, and best practices for managing cybersecurity risk.

Organizations of all sizes use the NIST CSF to build and improve cybersecurity programs.

---

# Purpose of the NIST CSF

The framework helps organizations:

- Identify cybersecurity risks.
- Protect critical assets.
- Detect security incidents.
- Respond effectively to cyberattacks.
- Recover from security incidents.
- Continuously improve security practices.

---

# NIST CSF Core Functions (Version 2.0)

## 1. Govern

Establish cybersecurity governance, strategy, policies, and risk management practices.

---

## 2. Identify

Understand organizational assets, systems, users, and cybersecurity risks.

Examples:

- Asset inventory
- Risk assessments
- Business environment analysis

---

## 3. Protect

Implement safeguards that reduce cybersecurity risks.

Examples:

- Access control
- Employee training
- Encryption
- Firewalls
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

## 4. Detect

Identify cybersecurity events as quickly as possible.

Examples:

- SIEM monitoring
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Security logging
- Threat intelligence

---

## 5. Respond

Take action to contain and mitigate security incidents.

Examples:

- Incident response plans
- Communication procedures
- Threat containment
- Digital forensics

---

## 6. Recover

Restore systems and operations after an incident.

Examples:

- System restoration
- Backup recovery
- Business continuity planning
- Lessons learned

---

# Insider Threats and Risk

One significant cybersecurity risk comes from **insider threats**.

Disgruntled employees can be particularly dangerous because they:

- Already have authorized access.
- Know where sensitive information is stored.
- Understand organizational systems.
- May misuse privileged access.

Organizations reduce insider risks by implementing:

- Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)
- Access monitoring
- User activity logging
- Security awareness training
- Regular permission reviews

---

# Benefits of Using the NIST CSF

Organizations that adopt the framework can:

- Improve cybersecurity posture.
- Reduce organizational risks.
- Protect valuable assets.
- Support regulatory compliance.
- Improve incident response.
- Strengthen long-term resilience.

---

# Key Takeaways

- The **CIA Triad** is the foundation of information security and consists of **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability**.
- **Confidentiality** protects information from unauthorized access.
- **Integrity** ensures data remains accurate, authentic, reliable, and unaltered.
- **Availability** ensures authorized users can access systems and data when needed.
- An **asset** is anything an organization considers valuable, and high-value assets require stronger security controls.
- The **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** is a voluntary framework that provides standards, guidelines, and best practices for managing cybersecurity risk.
- The six core functions of the NIST CSF are **Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover**.
- Organizations use the NIST CSF to manage both short-term and long-term cybersecurity risks while protecting critical assets from external and insider threats.
