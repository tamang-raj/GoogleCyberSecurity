# Security Frameworks, Controls, and Compliance

Organizations use **security frameworks**, **security controls**, and **compliance regulations** together to manage cybersecurity risks, protect sensitive information, and meet legal requirements.

Although these concepts are closely related, each serves a different purpose.

---


Together, they help reduce cybersecurity risks.

---

# The CIA Triad

The **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) Triad** is the foundational model used to evaluate cybersecurity risks.

| Principle | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Confidentiality** | Prevent unauthorized access to information. |
| **Integrity** | Ensure data remains accurate, authentic, and reliable. |
| **Availability** | Ensure authorized users can access systems and data when needed. |

Organizations use the CIA Triad when designing systems, implementing security controls, and developing security policies.

---

# Security Frameworks

## Definition

Security frameworks are structured guidelines that help organizations build cybersecurity programs and reduce risks to data and privacy.

They provide an organized approach to managing security throughout an organization's lifecycle.

---

## Core Components of Security Frameworks

### 1. Identify and Document Security Goals

Organizations define cybersecurity objectives based on:

- Business goals
- Legal requirements
- Industry standards
- Organizational risks

---

### 2. Set Guidelines

Organizations develop policies and standards to achieve their security goals.

Examples:

- Password policies
- Data classification policies
- Access control policies

---

### 3. Implement Strong Security Processes

Organizations establish repeatable procedures to protect systems and data.

Examples:

- Employee security training
- Backup procedures
- Incident response plans
- Access management

---

### 4. Monitor and Communicate Results

Organizations continuously evaluate security controls and report findings.

Activities include:

- Reviewing security logs
- Monitoring SIEM alerts
- Conducting audits
- Reporting incidents

---

# Security Controls

## Definition

Security controls are safeguards designed to reduce specific cybersecurity risks.

They are the practical mechanisms used to implement a security framework.

---

## Types of Security Controls

### Administrative Controls

Policies and procedures that guide employee behavior.

Examples:

- Security awareness training
- Acceptable use policies
- Incident response plans

---

### Technical Controls

Technology-based safeguards.

Examples:

- Firewalls
- Antivirus software
- Encryption
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- SIEM systems

---

### Physical Controls

Protect physical facilities and equipment.

Examples:

- Security guards
- Access badges
- Surveillance cameras
- Locked server rooms

---

# Compliance

## Definition

**Compliance** is the process of following applicable laws, regulations, standards, and organizational policies.

Compliance demonstrates that an organization meets required cybersecurity and privacy obligations.

---

# Major Security Frameworks and Compliance Standards

## NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)

### Purpose

Develops cybersecurity standards, guidelines, and frameworks used by organizations to manage cybersecurity risks.

### Key Frameworks

- **Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**
- **Risk Management Framework (RMF)**

### Characteristics

- Voluntary
- Risk-based
- Widely adopted
- Developed by the U.S. government

---

## NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

The CSF organizes cybersecurity activities into six core functions:

1. Govern
2. Identify
3. Protect
4. Detect
5. Respond
6. Recover

The framework helps organizations continuously improve cybersecurity.

---

## Risk Management Framework (RMF)

The RMF provides a structured process for managing cybersecurity risks throughout a system's lifecycle.

Organizations use the RMF to:

- Identify risks
- Assess risks
- Implement controls
- Monitor security continuously

---

# FERC–NERC

**Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)**

**North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC)**

### Purpose

Protects the electrical power grid across North America.

Organizations responsible for critical infrastructure must comply with:

- Critical Infrastructure Protection (CIP) Reliability Standards
- Security incident reporting requirements
- Risk management procedures

---

# FedRAMP

**Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program**

### Purpose

Standardizes the security assessment and authorization process for cloud services used by U.S. federal agencies.

### Benefits

- Consistent cloud security requirements
- Continuous monitoring
- Standardized authorization process

---

# CIS

**Center for Internet Security**

### Purpose

A nonprofit organization that develops best practices for improving cybersecurity.

### Provides

- CIS Controls
- CIS Benchmarks
- Incident response guidance
- Security recommendations

The CIS Controls provide prioritized actions organizations can take to strengthen cybersecurity.

---

# GDPR

**General Data Protection Regulation**

### Purpose

Protects the personal data and privacy of individuals residing in the European Union (EU).

### Key Requirements

- Protect personal information
- Obtain user consent
- Report breaches promptly
- Allow individuals to access and delete their personal data

Organizations that process EU citizens' data must comply with GDPR, regardless of where they are located.

---

# PCI DSS

**Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard**

### Purpose

Protects payment card information and reduces credit card fraud.

### Applies To

Organizations that:

- Store
- Process
- Transmit

credit or debit card information.

### Requirements

- Encrypt payment data
- Restrict access
- Monitor networks
- Perform vulnerability assessments

---

# HIPAA

**Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act**

### Purpose

A U.S. federal law that protects patients' health information.

### HIPAA Rules

- Privacy Rule
- Security Rule
- Breach Notification Rule

### Protects

- Medical records
- Health insurance information
- Patient diagnoses
- Treatment history

---

# ISO

**International Organization for Standardization**

### Purpose

Develops international standards that improve quality, efficiency, safety, and security.

### Cybersecurity Standard

**ISO/IEC 27001**

Provides requirements for establishing an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

---

# SOC Reports

**System and Organization Controls (SOC)**

Developed by the **American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA).**

### Purpose

Evaluate an organization's controls related to:

- Security
- Availability
- Confidentiality
- Privacy
- Processing integrity

SOC reports help customers assess whether service providers adequately protect sensitive information.

---

# Executive Order 14028

**Executive Order on Improving the Nation's Cybersecurity**

### Purpose

Issued by the U.S. government to strengthen national cybersecurity.

### Focus Areas

- Improving federal cybersecurity
- Strengthening software supply chain security
- Enhancing incident response
- Increasing information sharing
- Improving critical infrastructure protection

# Key Takeaways

- The **CIA Triad** provides the foundation for protecting information through **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability**.
- **Security frameworks** provide structured guidance for managing cybersecurity risks.
- **Security controls** are safeguards that implement the requirements defined by security frameworks.
- **Compliance** ensures organizations follow applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.
- **NIST**, **CIS**, and **ISO** provide widely adopted cybersecurity frameworks and best practices.
- **GDPR**, **HIPAA**, and **PCI DSS** establish legal or industry requirements for protecting sensitive information.
- **FedRAMP**, **FERC–NERC**, **SOC Reports**, and **Executive Order 14028** address specialized areas such as cloud security, critical infrastructure, compliance reporting, and national cybersecurity.
- Together, frameworks, controls, and compliance requirements help organizations reduce risk, protect assets, and maintain trust.
