# Computer Networks: Introduction

## Overview

A **computer network** is a group of interconnected devices that communicate with one another to share data, resources, and services. Networks enable everything from browsing the internet to sharing files and printing documents within an organization.

---

# What is a Network?

A **network** is a collection of connected devices capable of communicating with each other.

### Examples of Network Devices
- 💻 Laptops
- 📱 Smartphones
- 🖨️ Printers
- 🖥️ Desktop computers
- 🗄️ Servers
- 📷 Smart cameras
- 🏠 Smart home devices (IoT)

Networks allow these devices to exchange information and share resources efficiently.

---

# How Devices Communicate

Every device connected to a network is identified using unique addresses.

## IP Address (Internet Protocol Address)

An **IP address** is a logical address assigned to a device that allows it to communicate over a network.

### Example
```
192.168.1.10
```

### Purpose
- Identifies a device on a network
- Enables communication between devices
- Used for routing data across networks

---

## MAC Address (Media Access Control Address)

A **MAC address** is a unique physical identifier assigned to a device's network interface card (NIC).

### Example
```
00:1A:2B:3C:4D:5E
```

### Purpose
- Identifies hardware on a local network
- Used for communication within a LAN
- Permanently assigned by the manufacturer

---

# Ways Devices Connect

Devices communicate through:

### Wired Connections
- Ethernet cables
- Faster speeds
- More reliable connection
- Common in offices and data centers

### Wireless Connections
- Wi-Fi
- Bluetooth
- Cellular networks
- Greater mobility and convenience

---

# Types of Networks

## Local Area Network (LAN)

A **Local Area Network (LAN)** connects devices within a small geographic area.

### Examples
- Home Wi-Fi
- Office network
- School computer lab
- College campus building

### Characteristics
- Small coverage area
- High-speed communication
- Privately managed
- Low latency

---

## Wide Area Network (WAN)

A **Wide Area Network (WAN)** connects multiple LANs across large geographic distances.

### Examples
- The Internet
- Corporate branch offices
- Government networks
- International business networks

### Characteristics
- Covers cities, countries, or continents
- Connects multiple LANs
- Uses internet service providers (ISPs)
- Higher latency than LANs

---

# LAN vs WAN

| Feature | LAN | WAN |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Coverage Area | Small (Home, Office, School) | Large (City, Country, World) |
| Speed | Faster | Slower than LAN |
| Ownership | Private | Multiple providers |
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Example | Home Wi-Fi | Internet |

# Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Network** | A collection of connected devices that communicate with each other. |
| **IP Address** | A logical address used to identify devices on a network. |
| **MAC Address** | A unique hardware address assigned to a network interface. |
| **LAN** | A network covering a small geographical area such as a home or office. |
| **WAN** | A network spanning a large geographical area, such as the internet. |

---

# Key Takeaways

- A **network** connects devices so they can communicate and share resources.
- Devices use **IP addresses** and **MAC addresses** to identify and communicate with each other.
- Communication can occur through **wired** or **wireless** connections.
- A **LAN** covers a small area like a home or office.
- A **WAN** connects networks across large geographical distances, with the **Internet** being the most common example.

---

# Summary

Computer networks allow devices to exchange information efficiently. Every device uses **IP** and **MAC addresses** for identification and communication. Networks are generally categorized into **LANs**, which serve small local areas, and **WANs**, which connect networks across vast distances. Understanding these networking fundamentals is essential for cybersecurity professionals, as secure communication begins with a strong understanding of how networks operate.
